Classification: What is the R&D tax credit in accounting terms?

The R&D tax credit under the Research Allowance Act (Forschungszulagengesetz, FZulG) is neither a subsidy nor a grant in the traditional sense. It is a tax credit (Section 4(2) FZulG) that is offset against the assessed income tax or corporate tax (Koerperschaftsteuer). If the R&D tax credit exceeds the tax liability, the difference is paid out directly to the company.

This classification has far-reaching consequences for the accounting treatment:

  • Not a grant: The R&D tax credit is not treated as an investment grant or expense subsidy. Therefore, the accounting rules for government grants in the narrow sense do not apply.
  • Relevant for income tax: The R&D tax credit increases taxable income as other operating income. It must be reported in the profit and loss statement.
  • Claim against the tax office: The R&D tax credit establishes a claim by the company against the tax office for offset or refund – comparable to a tax refund receivable.
  • Legal basis: The entitlement arises by operation of law as soon as the requirements of the FZulG are met and the tax office assesses the credit.

For the R&D tax credit, it is therefore crucial to know the correct timing of recognition and the right accounts. Errors in booking can lead to objections during tax audits and, in the worst case, trigger reclaim demands.

Timing of recognition

One of the most common questions regarding the booking of the R&D tax credit concerns the correct timing of recognition in the accounts. There are two possible reference points that are frequently confused in practice:

After BSFZ certificate

The Certification Body for R&D Tax Credit (Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage, BSFZ) confirms the R&D nature of the projects. This certificate is a necessary prerequisite for the R&D tax credit but does not yet establish a legal entitlement. The BSFZ certificate alone therefore does not justify recognition of a receivable in the balance sheet.

After assessment notice from the tax office

The entitlement to the R&D tax credit only arises upon assessment by the tax office (Festsetzungsbescheid). Only when the assessment notice is available is the entitlement sufficiently concrete and legally secured to recognize a receivable in the balance sheet.

Recommendation: Book the R&D tax credit only upon receipt of the assessment notice. Premature recognition based solely on the BSFZ certificate or the application filed with the tax office carries the risk of incorrect accounting – particularly if the tax office reduces or rejects the requested credit. In case of doubt, a note in the appendix can refer to the expected R&D tax credit.

In practice, this means: The R&D tax credit is typically recognized as income in the fiscal year of the assessment – not in the fiscal year in which the R&D expenditures were incurred. If, for example, the assessment notice for fiscal year 2024 is only issued in 2026, the R&D tax credit is booked in the 2026 annual financial statements.

Journal entries: SKR03 and SKR04

The following journal entries show the correct booking of the R&D tax credit in the two most common German charts of accounts. The principle is the same in each case: Receivable to other operating income.

Journal entry upon assessment

Booking step SKR03 SKR04 Debit / Credit
Recognize receivable Account 1450 – Other assets Account 1590 – Other assets Debit
Record income Account 2749 – Other operating income Account 7695 – Other operating income Credit
Example journal entry (SKR03):

R&D tax credit assessed at EUR 150,000:

Account 1450 (Other assets)    EUR 150,000    to    Account 2749 (Other operating income)    EUR 150,000

Journal entry upon offset / payout

When the tax office offsets the R&D tax credit against the tax liability or pays out the surplus, the receivable is derecognized:

Booking step SKR03 SKR04 Debit / Credit
Offset against tax liability Account 1780 – VAT prior year (or income/corporate tax account) Account 3820 – (or income/corporate tax account) Debit
Derecognize receivable Account 1450 – Other assets Account 1590 – Other assets Credit
Or: Bank receipt upon refund Account 1200 – Bank Account 1800 – Bank Debit

HGB vs. IFRS: Differences in accounting treatment

Depending on the applicable accounting standard, there are differences in the treatment of the R&D tax credit. Both standards ultimately lead to income recognition – however, the timing and presentation may differ.

HGB accounting (German GAAP)

Under the German Commercial Code (HGB), the R&D tax credit is reported as other operating income in the profit and loss statement (Section 275 HGB). Recognition generally occurs in the fiscal year of the assessment by the tax office. By analogy with IAS 20 provisions, the R&D tax credit may be treated as an income grant, whereby it does not directly reduce personnel expenses but is presented as a separate income item.

  • Presentation: Other operating income (P&L)
  • Timing: Upon sufficient certainty of the entitlement (typically the assessment notice)
  • Notes: Disclosure of the R&D tax credit in the notes is recommended

IFRS accounting (IAS 20)

Under IFRS, the R&D tax credit must be accounted for under IAS 20 – Government Grants. IAS 20 requires that the grant be recognized only when there is reasonable assurance that the entity will comply with the conditions and that the grant will be received.

  • Gross method: Presentation as a separate income item in the P&L (similar to HGB)
  • Net method: Offset against the related expenses (personnel costs) – R&D costs are presented net
  • Timing: IFRS may allow earlier recognition than HGB if the conditions are sufficiently met before the assessment notice
Criterion HGB IFRS (IAS 20)
Principle Income recognition Income recognition
Presentation Other operating income Gross or net method (choice)
Timing Assessment notice Reasonable assurance (potentially earlier)
Netting Not permitted (gross principle) Net method permitted

Special considerations for corporate tax (Koerperschaftsteuer) and trade tax (Gewerbesteuer)

The tax treatment of the R&D tax credit has important special features that must be taken into account when booking and filing tax returns.

Trade tax exemption (Section 4(3) FZulG)

The R&D tax credit is exempt from trade tax (Gewerbesteuer). Under Section 4(3) FZulG, the R&D tax credit is not added to trade income. This means: Although the R&D tax credit is reported as other operating income in both the commercial and tax balance sheets, it must be deducted again when calculating trade income.

In practice, this requires an off-balance-sheet adjustment in the trade tax return. The income from the R&D tax credit is deducted as a reduction from trade income.

Offset against income tax / corporate tax (Koerperschaftsteuer)

The R&D tax credit is offset against the assessed income tax or corporate tax (Koerperschaftsteuer). The following applies:

  • Offset: The R&D tax credit reduces the income tax or corporate tax payable for the respective assessment period
  • Refund: If the R&D tax credit exceeds the tax liability, the difference is refunded – the credit can therefore result in a net payout
  • No offset against trade tax: The R&D tax credit is not offset against trade tax
  • Solidarity surcharge: The R&D tax credit is applied before the solidarity surcharge
Important: Do not forget the trade tax reduction! If the R&D tax credit is mistakenly left in trade income, you will pay trade tax on the R&D tax credit – even though you are legally exempt. More on the tax treatment can be found on our corporate tax (Koerperschaftsteuer) and R&D tax credit page.

Common errors in booking

In practice, errors regularly occur when booking the R&D tax credit that can lead to objections during tax audits. The following errors should definitely be avoided:

The 5 most common errors when booking the R&D tax credit:

1. Recognized too early: The receivable is recognized as early as the BSFZ certificate or the application to the tax office, even though the assessment notice is still pending. This can lead to an overvaluation of assets.

2. Wrong accounts used: The R&D tax credit is booked to tax refund accounts instead of other operating income. The correct treatment is to record it as income (Account 2749 / 7695), not as a tax refund.

3. Trade tax reduction forgotten: The off-balance-sheet reduction for trade tax (Gewerbesteuer) is not made. The R&D tax credit then incorrectly remains in trade income and is subject to trade tax.

4. Prior year correction not considered: If the assessment notice arrives in a subsequent year, the correction is recorded as prior-period income. A note in the appendix is recommended in this case.

5. No reconciliation between commercial and tax balance sheet: Different approaches in the commercial and tax balance sheets are not documented. With different recognition timings (e.g., HGB vs. IFRS), deferred taxes arise that must be correctly recorded.

Practical tip: Coordination with your tax advisor

Coordination is key: The R&D tax credit affects multiple areas simultaneously – bookkeeping, financial reporting, tax returns and trade tax (Gewerbesteuer). Close coordination between your tax advisor, accounting department and, where applicable, auditor is essential to avoid errors.

Our recommendation:
- Inform your tax advisor early about the R&D tax credit application
- Clarify the account assignment (SKR03 or SKR04) in advance
- Ensure that the trade tax reduction is taken into account in the tax return
- Document the timing of the assessment and the booking date
- For IFRS reporters: Determine the method (gross vs. net) consistently
