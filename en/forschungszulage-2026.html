Timeline: When Each Change Took Effect

From the introduction in 2020 to the full changes in 2026 – the development of the R&D Tax Credit at a glance.

2020

R&D Tax Credit Takes Effect

Funding rate of 25% on eligible R&D expenditure. Maximum assessment basis: EUR 2M per fiscal year. Retroactive application from 01.01.2020 possible.

2021

Assessment Basis Increased

As part of the COVID economic stimulus measures, the maximum assessment basis was doubled from EUR 2M to EUR 4M. Maximum allowance thereby EUR 1M.

2024

Growth Opportunities Act

Two key improvements: The SME bonus raises the funding rate for small and medium-sized enterprises to 35%. The assessment basis increases to EUR 12M, enabling a maximum R&D Tax Credit of EUR 4.2M.

2025

Overhead Surcharge Introduced

New: A flat surcharge of 20% for overhead costs can be applied to eligible personnel costs. No individual documentation required – the assessment basis increases automatically.

2026

All Changes Fully Effective

All enacted improvements are now fully in effect. Companies can also submit retroactive applications back to 2020, provided the tax assessment is still open. The ideal time to maximize your R&D funding.

Who Benefits Most from the Changes?

The R&D Tax Credit reforms benefit various types of companies. These four groups benefit the most.

SMEs with R&D Department

Small and medium-sized enterprises benefit the most: The increased funding rate of 35% instead of 25% means 40% more funding with identical R&D expenditure. Combined with the overhead surcharge, the effect is amplified further.

Companies with High Personnel Costs

With the tripling of the assessment basis to EUR 12M, the R&D Tax Credit is now particularly worthwhile for companies with extensive R&D teams. Previously, the cap was EUR 4M – this cap effectively no longer applies to most companies.

Companies Without Previous Application

Companies that have never applied for the R&D Tax Credit can now retroactively claim for 2020 to 2025. This means potentially six fiscal years of funding at once – an enormous financial lever for innovation-driven companies.

Companies with Contract Research

Even with outsourced development you benefit: 60% of contract research costs are eligible, and the 20% overhead surcharge can additionally be applied to the personnel component. The higher assessment basis also comes into play.

In Detail

Detailed Changes Overview

The Growth Opportunities Act (Wachstumschancengesetz) and subsequent adjustments have fundamentally reformed Germany's R&D Tax Credit. Here are the individual changes in detail.

1 Increased Funding Rate for SMEs: From 25% to 35%

The most significant change of the Growth Opportunities Act for small businesses: Small and medium-sized enterprises under the EU definition (fewer than 250 employees, maximum EUR 50M annual turnover or EUR 43M balance sheet total) receive a funding rate of 35% instead of the previous 25% from fiscal year 2024 onwards. In concrete terms: For every EUR 100,000 in eligible R&D expenditure, the tax credit is no longer EUR 25,000 but EUR 35,000.

Large enterprises retain the standard rate of 25%. Whether a company qualifies as an SME is assessed at the time of application. Affiliated companies are aggregated – a point many applicants underestimate.

2 Assessment Basis Tripled: From EUR 4M to EUR 12M

Until 2023, the maximum assessment basis was EUR 4M per fiscal year – originally only EUR 2M. The Growth Opportunities Act raised this cap to EUR 12M. This refers to the total amount of eligible expenditure a company can claim per year.

In practice, this change primarily benefits larger companies with substantial R&D departments. A company with EUR 8M in annual R&D personnel costs was previously capped at EUR 4M – now the full amount is considered. That alone doubles the credit in such cases.

3 Maximum R&D Tax Credit: Up to EUR 4.2M Per Year

Combining the increased assessment basis (EUR 12M) with the SME bonus (35%) yields a theoretical maximum R&D Tax Credit of EUR 4.2M per fiscal year. For large enterprises at the standard rate of 25%, the maximum is EUR 3M. For comparison: under the old rules, the maximum was EUR 1M. The increase is more than fourfold – a genuine paradigm shift in Germany's tax-based R&D incentive system.

4 Material Costs Now Eligible Since 2024

Before 2024, only personnel costs and contract research expenses were eligible. The Growth Opportunities Act now includes material costs (Sachkosten) in the assessment basis, provided they can be directly attributed to an eligible R&D project. This covers items such as prototype materials, laboratory consumables, or specialised software licences for research operations. Documentation requires project-specific allocation – blanket cost allocation is not permitted.

5 Owner Contributions: EUR 100/Hour at Max. 40 Hours/Week

For sole proprietors and shareholders who are personally involved in R&D projects, a flat hourly rate of EUR 100 applies – up to a maximum of 40 hours per week. At full capacity, this yields an annual assessment basis of approximately EUR 208,000 per person. As an SME with the 35% rate, that translates to up to EUR 72,800 in R&D Tax Credit per year, solely from the owner's own work. The prerequisite is proper time tracking that documents the share of R&D activity relative to total working time.

By Company Size

Impact by Company Size

The reforms affect companies differently depending on their size and structure. Here is an overview of what the changes mean in practice.

Startups and Sole Proprietors

Founders and sole proprietors benefit primarily from the owner contribution rule: EUR 100 per hour, up to 40 hours per week. A managing director who is also the sole shareholder and personally conducts R&D can claim up to EUR 208,000 in assessment basis per year. At the SME rate of 35%, that yields a credit of around EUR 72,800 – funds that can be decisive in the early stages of a company.

Another key point: Startups often incur losses in their first years. The R&D Tax Credit is granted regardless – it is offset against the tax liability or paid out directly if there is no tax liability. This makes it a genuine financing instrument even for pre-profit companies.

SMEs (Under 250 Employees)

For the classic Mittelstand, several improvements converge: the increased funding rate of 35%, the tripled assessment basis, the inclusion of material costs, and the 20% overhead surcharge. An SME with 15 R&D employees and EUR 1.2M in annual personnel costs previously received EUR 300,000 (25% of EUR 1.2M). Under the new rules: EUR 1.2M + EUR 240,000 overhead = EUR 1.44M assessment basis, at 35% = EUR 504,000 in R&D Tax Credit. That is a 68% increase.

Critical for SMEs: Classification as a small or medium-sized enterprise under the EU SME definition is binding. Companies that exceed the thresholds due to growth or shareholding structures revert to the standard rate of 25%. Regular review is worthwhile.

Large Enterprises (250+ Employees)

For large enterprises, the funding rate remains at 25%, but the tripling of the assessment basis from EUR 4M to EUR 12M makes a substantial difference. A company that previously hit the EUR 4M cap with EUR 8M in R&D expenditure can now claim the full EUR 8M. The credit doubles in this scenario, from EUR 1M to EUR 2M.

The overhead surcharge is also relevant for corporations: 20% on R&D personnel costs flows into the assessment basis without itemised documentation. For an R&D department with EUR 6M in personnel costs, that is an additional EUR 1.2M – translating to EUR 300,000 more in R&D Tax Credit per year.

The various reforms took effect at different times. Special rules apply for retroactive claims.

When Did Each Change Take Effect?

From FY 2020 R&D Tax Credit available (25% rate, EUR 2M assessment basis)
From FY 2021 Assessment basis raised to EUR 4M (COVID economic stimulus package)

From FY 2025 20% overhead surcharge on personnel costs

Retroactive Claims

A frequently overlooked advantage: The R&D Tax Credit can be claimed retroactively – for any fiscal year where the tax assessment has not yet become final (bestandskräftig). In practice, this means: If you have never filed a claim, you may be able to claim the R&D Tax Credit for fiscal years 2020 through 2025 in 2026.

The application to the Certification Body for R&D Tax Credit (Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage, BSFZ) is the first step: They assess whether your R&D projects meet the substantive requirements. After receiving the certificate, you submit the tax application to your responsible tax office.

Important: There is no exclusion deadline for the BSFZ application, but the statute of limitations for tax assessment limits the time window. Companies that have conducted R&D activities since 2020 and have not yet filed a claim should act promptly before older fiscal years fall outside the assessment period.
