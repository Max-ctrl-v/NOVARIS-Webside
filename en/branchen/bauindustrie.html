When does your project qualify?

FZulG Criteria for
Construction Projects

Technological Novelty

The process, material or system goes beyond standard construction technology and established planning standards.

Technical Uncertainty

Component tests, material trials and iterative prototype development document that success was not foreseeable.

Systematic Approach

Test logs, inspection reports (e.g. DIN/EN tests) and development documentation document the scientific methodology.

No Routine Planning

Regular architectural and engineering services as well as standard BIM application are not eligible – only genuine R&D activities count.

Important to know

Even prototype and pilot buildings as demonstrators for new construction technologies are eligible – provided their primary purpose is knowledge generation and not regular use.

Eligible are: BIM developers, materials researchers, robotics engineers as well as contract research at universities and research institutes (60%).

Typical funding per Construction project: €50,000 – €200,000/year

Industry-Specific

Industry-Specific Requirements in the Construction Industry

The construction industry is often not perceived as a traditional R&D sector – unjustly so. Building Information Modeling (BIM) is emerging as a central driver of construction technology innovation. Development of proprietary BIM plugins, parametric design algorithms, digital twins for buildings, and AI-driven planning optimization generates substantial eligible R&D costs. If your company develops custom BIM workflows that go beyond merely using commercial software (Revit, ArchiCAD, Allplan), strong funding opportunities exist.

Development and testing of novel construction materials is a core eligible area. Ultra-high-performance concrete (UHPC), textile-reinforced concrete, self-healing concretes with bacterial inclusions, lightweight materials from recycled sources, and bio-based insulation materials require systematic test series for mechanical property determination, durability testing, and fire resistance certification. Testing per DIN EN 206 (concrete), DIN EN 1992 (reinforced concrete), and DIN 4108 (thermal protection) generates extensive test documentation suitable as R&D evidence.

Structural simulations and load-bearing optimization with FEM software constitute eligible R&D when they go beyond standard calculations. Development of novel structural concepts (topology-optimized structures, adaptive facade systems, modular hybrid construction), simulation of complex soil-structure interactions, and numerical modeling of innovative connection techniques (adhesive bonding in construction, integrated sensor systems) require experimental validation and iterative optimization cycles.

Sustainability R&D is gaining increasing importance in construction and offers substantial funding potential. Development of low-carbon cement formulations, circular construction designs (Design for Disassembly), energy-autonomous building concepts, and innovative building services (e.g., ice storage, thermally activated building systems with heat pumps) are typical eligible projects. Development of automated construction processes – masonry robotics, 3D concrete printing, drone-based site monitoring – also regularly qualifies for the research allowance.
