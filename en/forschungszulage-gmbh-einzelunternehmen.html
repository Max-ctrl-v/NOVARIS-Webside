Every Taxable Company Is Eligible – Regardless of Legal Form

The R&D Tax Credit under the Research Allowance Act (FZulG) is generally available to every company with unlimited tax liability in Germany – regardless of the chosen legal form, company size, or industry. This is clearly regulated in §1 FZulG and applied accordingly by the tax authorities.

In detail, this means: all companies subject to either corporate tax (corporations such as GmbH, UG, AG) or income tax (sole proprietorships, partnerships such as KG, GbR, OHG) in Germany are entitled. The R&D Tax Credit is not a subsidy with a limited recipient group but a tax entitlement available to every taxable company that conducts qualified R&D projects.

Eligible legal forms: GmbH, UG (limited liability), AG, KG, GmbH & Co. KG, OHG, GbR, sole proprietorship, registered cooperative (eG), foundation (commercial sector), and permanent establishments of foreign companies in Germany.

Although the entitlement exists regardless of legal form, the practical implementation differs significantly depending on the legal form. The key differences concern three areas: First, who submits the application (the company itself or the individual partners). Second, whether owner contributions by the owner or partners are eligible. Third, how the R&D Tax Credit is offset against tax liability (through the corporate tax or income tax assessment).

These differences can significantly impact the amount of R&D Tax Credit actually received. Especially for owner-managed companies and partnerships, there are structuring options that do not exist for corporations – and vice versa. Therefore, it is worthwhile to know the specifics of your own legal form and optimize the application accordingly.

Research Allowance for GmbH, UG, and AG

Corporations – i.e., GmbH, UG (limited liability), and AG – are the most common legal form among R&D Tax Credit applicants. The application process is relatively straightforward here, as the company itself is the applicant and the R&D Tax Credit is directly offset against corporate tax.

Application by the Company

For corporations, the company itself files the R&D Tax Credit application. The application is first submitted to the BSFZ (Federal Certification Body for Research Allowance) for certification of R&D projects and then to the responsible tax office for assessment of the R&D Tax Credit. The company acts through its authorized representatives (managing directors for GmbH/UG, board of directors for AG).

Eligible Expenditures

For corporations, the following expenditures are eligible:

  • Personnel costs of employed R&D staff: Gross wages including employer social security contributions for all employees working on eligible R&D projects
  • Managing director's salary: The managing director's salary is eligible if they demonstrably perform R&D activities themselves. The key is proper time tracking documenting the R&D portion of the managing director's work
  • Contract research: 60% of costs for R&D contracts awarded to third parties (maximum EUR 2 million assessment basis per fiscal year)
  • Overhead allowance: From 2026, an additional 20% on eligible personnel costs

Owner Contributions: The Important Restriction

For corporations, owner contributions by shareholders are not eligible. The reason: shareholders of a GmbH or AG are not employees of the company in the tax sense. Even if a shareholder-managing director actively works in R&D, they cannot claim their working time as owner contributions (with the EUR 40 flat rate). Instead, a social security-liable employment relationship must exist for the managing director's salary to be eligible as personnel costs.

Practical tip for GmbH owners: If you are a managing shareholder actively working in R&D, ensure that a proper employment contract with appropriate salary exists. Maintain detailed time tracking of your R&D activities. This way, you can claim your managing director's salary proportionally as eligible personnel costs – which often leads to higher funding than the owner contribution flat rate.

Tax Offsetting

For corporations, the R&D Tax Credit is assessed and offset through the corporate tax assessment. The credit is applied against the assessed corporate tax (including solidarity surcharge). If the R&D Tax Credit exceeds the assessed tax, the difference is paid out as a tax refund. This is particularly relevant for startups and companies in loss-making phases: even without a tax liability, you receive the full R&D Tax Credit as a payout.

Research Allowance for Sole Proprietors (Einzelunternehmen)

Sole proprietorships and freelancers hold a special position regarding the R&D Tax Credit, as they benefit from a provision not available to corporations: the eligibility of the owner's contributions.

The Key Advantage: Owner Contributions Are Eligible

As a sole proprietor, you can include your own R&D working time as owner contributions in the assessment basis for the R&D Tax Credit. The law provides a flat rate of EUR 40 per hour for this purpose. A maximum of 40 hours per week can be claimed. For year-round R&D activity, this results in an additional assessment basis of up to EUR 83,200 per year (EUR 40 x 40 h x 52 weeks).

At a funding rate of 25% (or 35% for SMEs), this means an R&D Tax Credit from owner contributions alone of up to EUR 20,800 (or EUR 29,120 for SMEs) – in addition to the personnel costs of employed staff. This owner contribution flat rate is particularly attractive for owner-managed companies where the owner is the central R&D actor.

Calculation example – Sole proprietor with one employee:

Owner contribution: EUR 40 x 30 h/week x 52 weeks = EUR 62,400
Employee personnel costs: EUR 55,000
Subtotal: EUR 117,400
+ 20% overhead allowance: EUR 23,480
= Assessment basis: EUR 140,880
x 35% (SME rate): = EUR 49,308 R&D Tax Credit

Additionally: Personnel Costs of Employed Staff

In addition to owner contributions, the personnel costs of employed R&D staff are naturally also eligible – under the same conditions as for corporations. The owner's contributions are therefore added as an additional assessment basis and increase the total funding.

Documentation Requirements

For recognition of owner contributions, project-related time tracking is essential. Document your daily R&D working time with reference to the certified research projects. Time tracking must be done promptly (ideally daily or weekly) and be plausible. Ensure that documented hours are consistent with actual project progress.

Tax Offsetting

For sole proprietorships, the R&D Tax Credit is offset through the owner's personal income tax assessment. The credit is applied against the assessed income tax. Here too: if the R&D Tax Credit exceeds the tax liability, the difference is paid out. This makes the R&D Tax Credit attractive even for sole proprietors in the startup phase or with low profits.

Important for freelancers: Freelance professionals (e.g., engineers, IT consultants, scientists) can also apply for the R&D Tax Credit, provided they conduct their own R&D projects. The owner contribution flat rate of EUR 40/h applies here as well. The prerequisite is that the R&D activity meets the FZulG criteria and is not merely a commissioned service for a client.

Research Allowance for KG, GbR, and OHG

Partnerships hold a special position regarding the R&D Tax Credit that frequently leads to uncertainties in practice. The basic rule is: the application is filed at the company level, but the allocation and tax offsetting of the R&D Tax Credit occurs at the level of individual partners.

Application and Allocation

For partnerships, the company files the BSFZ application for certification of R&D projects. However, the subsequent assessment of the R&D Tax Credit occurs at the partner level: each partner receives their share according to the contractual profit distribution. The R&D Tax Credit is then offset in each partner's individual income tax assessment.

Owner Contributions by Partners

The eligibility of owner contributions for partnerships depends on the tax classification of the partner. As a general rule: partners who qualify as co-entrepreneurs and are themselves active in R&D can claim owner contributions – under the same conditions as sole proprietors (EUR 40/h flat rate, maximum 40 hours per week).

For GbR and OHG, partners are generally co-entrepreneurs, so owner contributions are eligible. For GmbH & Co. KG, the situation is more nuanced: the general partner GmbH cannot claim owner contributions (as it is a corporation). However, natural persons among the limited partners can claim owner contributions under certain conditions, provided they are actually active in R&D and classified as co-entrepreneurs.

GmbH & Co. KG: Special Complexity

The GmbH & Co. KG is the most complex legal form in the context of the R&D Tax Credit. Here, the regulations for corporations (general partner GmbH) and partnerships (KG) intersect. The key specifics:

  • Personnel costs: Employees employed by the KG generate eligible personnel costs at the KG level
  • Managing director of the general partner GmbH: Their salary can be eligible if they perform R&D activities for the KG – the tax allocation is decisive here
  • Limited partners: Owner contributions are only eligible if the limited partner qualifies as a co-entrepreneur and actually performs R&D
  • Distribution: The R&D Tax Credit is distributed among the partners of the KG, not to the general partner GmbH as a separate legal entity
Our recommendation: For partnerships – especially for GmbH & Co. KG – we strongly recommend professional advisory. The intersection of company law, tax law, and the Research Allowance Act contains numerous pitfalls that can lead to avoidable funding losses or, in the worst case, erroneous applications.

Special Cases and Common Pitfalls

Beyond the common legal forms, there are numerous special cases that regularly arise in practice and require particular attention:

Affiliated Companies: Carefully Verify SME Status

The increased SME funding rate of 35% instead of 25% makes a significant difference in funding amounts. However, SME status is assessed at group level: partner companies and affiliated companies are proportionally or fully counted when calculating employee numbers and revenue. A company with 50 employees can thus lose its SME status if it is part of a larger corporate group. Carefully review your shareholding structure against the EU SME definition.

Tax Group (Organschaft): Only the Parent Company Applies

In a corporate tax group (Organschaft), only the parent company (Organträger) applies for the R&D Tax Credit. The R&D expenditures of the subsidiaries are attributed to the parent company. This can have both advantages and disadvantages: on one hand, expenditures are pooled; on the other hand, all group members share the maximum assessment basis of EUR 12 million. Dissolving the tax group may be advantageous in certain circumstances – but this should be carefully weighed against other tax implications.

Permanent Establishments of Foreign Companies

Foreign companies maintaining a permanent establishment in Germany can also apply for the R&D Tax Credit – provided the permanent establishment is subject to tax in Germany. However, only R&D expenditures attributable to the German permanent establishment are eligible. Personnel costs for employees working abroad are not eligible, even if they work on a German project. The allocation follows the general profit attribution rules for permanent establishments.

Non-Profit Organizations

Non-profit organizations such as associations, foundations, or non-profit GmbHs (gGmbH) can apply for the R&D Tax Credit only for their taxable commercial operations. R&D expenditures in the non-profit or charitable sector are not eligible. In practice, the distinction is often difficult and requires careful tax classification.

New Businesses and Startups

Good news for founders: the R&D Tax Credit is available to your company from the very first fiscal year – regardless of whether revenues have already been generated. Since the R&D Tax Credit is also paid out in case of losses (it is a tax credit, not a tax reduction), it is particularly valuable for startups in the development phase. You receive the R&D Tax Credit as a direct payment from the tax office, thereby improving your cash flow during the most critical business phase.

Common pitfall when changing legal form: When your company changes its legal form (e.g., from sole proprietorship to GmbH), pending BSFZ applications and R&D Tax Credit entitlements must be transferred to the legal successor. Notify both the BSFZ and the tax office of the change. The assessment basis is generally allocated on a pro-rata basis between the old and new legal form. Note: owner contributions that were eligible as a sole proprietor are no longer claimable after converting to a GmbH.
