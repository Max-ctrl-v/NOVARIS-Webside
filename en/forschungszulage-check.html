Good to Know

R&D Tax Credit Requirements in Detail

Legal Eligibility Requirements (Section 1 FZulG)

The R&D Tax Credit under the Research Allowance Act (FZulG) is generally available to all companies that are subject to unlimited or limited income or corporate tax liability in Germany. This means: sole proprietorships, partnerships (GbR, OHG, KG), corporations (GmbH, AG, UG), and even branches of foreign companies with German permanent establishments can apply for the R&D Tax Credit. The legal form is irrelevant, as are company size and industry. The only decisive factor is that the company conducts its own or commissioned research and development projects under Section 2 FZulG and is subject to taxation in Germany.

Companies that currently have no profits also benefit from the R&D Tax Credit. Since the credit is directly offset against the tax liability and paid out as a refund in case of a surplus, startups and companies in their growth phase receive an immediate liquidity improvement. This makes the FZulG one of the most attractive funding instruments for research-active companies in Germany.

What Counts as Research and Development? (Section 2 FZulG)

The FZulG defines eligible research and development projects in accordance with the OECD Frascati Manual. Projects are eligible if they aim at acquiring new knowledge and meet one of the following three criteria: they constitute basic research, industrial research, or experimental development. A project must be conducted systematically, methodically, and transparently. Technical uncertainty must also exist that cannot be resolved through the mere application of existing knowledge.

Specific examples of eligible projects include the development of novel software algorithms, research into new materials, the design of innovative machine components, the development of new pharmaceutical active ingredients, or the testing of environmentally friendly production processes. Projects in which existing products or processes are significantly improved can also be eligible, provided that technical challenges must be overcome.

The Three R&D Categories Explained

  • Basic Research: Experimental or theoretical work to acquire new knowledge without a specific practical application in focus. Example: Research into fundamental physical properties of new material composites.
  • Industrial Research: Systematic research to acquire new knowledge with the aim of developing new products, processes, or services or significantly improving existing ones. Example: Development of a new sensor concept for autonomous driving.
  • Experimental Development: Acquiring, combining, and using existing knowledge and skills to produce new or significantly improved products, processes, or services. Example: Construction and testing of a prototype for a novel heat pump.

What Does NOT Count as Eligible R&D?

Not every technical activity automatically qualifies as R&D under the FZulG. Explicitly excluded are:

  • Routine modifications to existing products, production lines, or processes
  • Quality controls and standard tests without a research character
  • Pure market research, sales activities, and feasibility studies
  • Training and education of employees
  • Administrative and organizational preparations for R&D
  • Maintenance and simple adjustments to existing software or machinery
  • Reverse engineering of existing products without a novelty aspect

Special Cases: Partnerships, Startups & Subsidiaries

Partnerships (GbR, OHG, KG) can also apply for the R&D Tax Credit. In this case, the credit is allocated proportionally to the partners, who claim it in their personal tax returns. For affiliated companies under EU state aid law, the maximum assessment basis of EUR 12M applies cumulatively to all affiliated companies combined. Startups benefit particularly: since the R&D Tax Credit is also paid out as a tax refund in the absence of profits, it immediately improves liquidity in the often capital-intensive start-up phase. German subsidiaries of foreign groups are also eligible, provided they are independently tax-liable and the R&D activities are carried out in Germany or commissioned from there.

Practical Tips

How to Maximize Your Funding

Identify ALL Qualifying Projects

Many companies underestimate how many of their projects actually qualify as R&D under the FZulG. The most commonly overlooked areas: software development with technical novelty, process optimization with an experimental character, product improvements with technical uncertainty, and cross-departmental innovation projects. A systematic analysis of all business areas by experienced consultants like NOVARIS Consulting regularly uncovers 30-50% more eligible projects than the initial internal assessment.

Use the Overhead Surcharge (20%)

A flat overhead surcharge of 20% can be applied to eligible personnel costs and owner contributions. This covers proportional costs for premises, equipment, software licenses, and consumables incurred in the course of R&D activities. You should always claim this surcharge, as it increases the assessment basis and thus the funding amount.

Retroactive Claims Back to 2020

The FZulG has been in effect since January 1, 2020. As long as the tax assessment for the respective fiscal year has not yet become final, you can apply for the R&D Tax Credit retroactively. In practice, this means: many companies can still make retroactive claims for the years 2020 to 2025. For a company with 10 R&D employees, the retroactive funding can quickly reach a six-figure amount. Early application is crucial, as tax assessments eventually become final.

Proper Time Tracking for R&D Personnel

Complete documentation of R&D working hours is a central requirement for a successful application. The tax office requires project-based time tracking that transparently demonstrates which employees spent how much time on eligible projects. NOVARIS Consulting supports you in setting up compliant time tracking and reviews your existing documentation for completeness and plausibility.
