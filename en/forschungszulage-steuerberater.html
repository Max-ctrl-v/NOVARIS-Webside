Why Many Tax Advisors Reach Their Limits with the Research Allowance

The R&D Tax Credit under the Research Allowance Act (FZulG) is a tax instrument – which suggests that the tax advisor is the natural point of contact. And indeed, the tax advisor plays an important role, particularly in integrating the R&D Tax Credit into the tax return and communicating with the tax office. However, reality shows: The FZulG application has two fundamentally different dimensions that require different competencies.

The first dimension is tax-related: Annex FZ, assessment basis, offsetting against tax liability, interaction with advance payments. This is where the tax advisor is in their element. The second dimension, however, is technical-scientific: The BSFZ certification (Federal Certification Body for Research Allowance) requires a detailed description of R&D projects that must demonstrate technical novelty and scientific uncertainty. And this is precisely where many tax advisors reach their limits.

This is not a criticism – it is in the nature of things. Tax advisors are experts in tax law, not in evaluating R&D projects according to Frascati Manual criteria. The consequence in practice:

  • Rejections by the BSFZ: The technical project description is too vague, too superficial, or does not use the correct terminology. The BSFZ does not recognize the project as eligible R&D.
  • Assessment basis too low: Not all eligible projects are identified. Many companies have more R&D than they think – but without a systematic analysis, projects and thus funding potential remain undiscovered.
  • Missed projects: The tax advisor knows the financial data but not the technical details of the projects. Without deep understanding of R&D activities, eligible projects can be overlooked.
  • Missing documentation: Time tracking and project documentation are not aligned with FZulG requirements. Without specialized consulting, crucial evidence is often missing.
Numbers from practice: Based on our experience, companies that submit the FZulG application without specialized support typically utilize only 40–60% of their actual funding potential. The most common reasons: unrecognized R&D projects, vague BSFZ descriptions, and missing documentation of personnel costs.

Tax Expertise + Technical R&D Knowledge = Maximum Research Allowance

The R&D Tax Credit process consists of two clearly separated pillars that require different professional competencies. Only when both pillars are professionally handled is maximum funding guaranteed.

Pillar 1: The BSFZ Application (Technical)

The Federal Certification Body (BSFZ) examines whether submitted projects meet the criteria for eligible research and development. The BSFZ application requires:

  • Technical project description: Detailed presentation of the project, the technical problem, the solution approach, and the targeted results
  • R&D categorization: Classification as basic research, industrial research, or experimental development
  • Proof of technical novelty: Demonstration of where the technical uncertainty and knowledge gain lie
  • Distinction from routine activities: Clear differentiation between R&D and normal business operations

These tasks require deep understanding of the respective technology and R&D methodology. A tax advisor typically does not have this expertise – and it is not their field either.

Pillar 2: The Tax Office Application (Tax)

After receiving the BSFZ certificate, the application is submitted to the responsible tax office. Here the tax advisor is indispensable:

  • Annex FZ: Correct integration into the income or corporate tax return
  • Assessment basis: Precise calculation of eligible expenditures (personnel costs, owner contributions, contract research, overhead allowance)
  • Tax assessment: Offsetting the R&D Tax Credit against tax liability or payout as refund
  • Communication with the tax office: Responding to inquiries and objections
Connecting the pillars: Both application parts must be consistent. The projects named in the BSFZ certificate must be reflected in Annex FZ, personnel costs must be allocated to projects, and time tracking must confirm both. Inconsistencies between the BSFZ certificate and the tax office application are one of the most common reasons for inquiries and delays.

5 Questions Your Tax Advisor Should Be Able to Answer

To assess whether your tax advisor can competently handle the R&D Tax Credit, ask them the following five questions. An experienced FZulG consultant knows the answers by heart. If your tax advisor hesitates on multiple questions, it is a strong indication that specialized support makes sense.

1 What is the difference between Annex FZ and the BSFZ certificate? – Annex FZ is the tax form for the tax office; the BSFZ certificate is the technical confirmation of R&D projects by the certification body. Both are separate procedures with different requirements.
2 How is the assessment basis calculated for in-house research? – Proportional gross wages of R&D employees (based on time spent) + 20% overhead allowance from 2026 + where applicable, owner-operator flat rate (EUR 70/h for shareholders). The upper limit is EUR 12 million per fiscal year.
3 What funding rate applies for SMEs vs. large enterprises? – SMEs receive 35% of eligible expenditures (since the Growth Opportunities Act 2024), large enterprises 25%. The SME definition follows the EU regulation (< 250 employees, < EUR 50 million turnover or < EUR 43 million balance sheet total).
4 How is the 20% overhead surcharge correctly applied? – From fiscal year 2026, 20% of eligible personnel costs and owner contributions can be included as a flat-rate overhead in the assessment basis. This does not require separate documentation of actual overhead costs.
5 Can the R&D Tax Credit be offset against advance tax payments? – Yes, the R&D Tax Credit is applied against the assessed tax. If the credit exceeds the tax liability, the difference is paid out. Offsetting against advance payments is possible within the scope of the tax assessment.
Important: These questions cover only the tax side. Technical competency – i.e., the ability to correctly identify R&D projects and prepare them for the BSFZ – is an additional skill set that very few tax advisors possess.

7 Signs You Need a Research Allowance Specialist

Not every company needs a specialized FZulG consultant. But if one or more of the following points apply to you, there is a high probability that you will achieve significantly higher funding with professional support than without.

1. Your tax advisor has never filed an FZulG application. The R&D Tax Credit has existed since 2020, but many tax advisors still have no practical experience with it. Without routine in dealing with the BSFZ and the specific application process, the risk of errors increases significantly.
2. Your BSFZ certificate was rejected. A rejection does not mean your projects are not eligible – it is often due to the wording of the project description. A specialized consultant knows the BSFZ criteria and can prepare your application so that eligibility is clearly recognizable.
3. You are unsure which projects are eligible. The distinction between R&D and routine development is often not trivial. Many companies underestimate their R&D share because they do not know that incremental improvements and process innovations can also be eligible.
4. You want to apply retroactively from 2020. Retroactive applications are particularly complex: past projects must be identified, personnel costs reconstructed, and time records prepared after the fact. Without an experienced consultant, significant amounts often remain unclaimed.
5. You have contract research that needs to be correctly allocated. For contract research, only 60% of the fee is eligible, and the contractor must be based in the EU/EEA. Correct allocation and documentation requires specialist knowledge.
6. Your funding was significantly lower than expected. If you feel that your R&D Tax Credit was too low, a second review is worthwhile. Often not all eligible projects are captured, or the assessment basis was calculated too conservatively.
7. You want to professionalize your R&D documentation. Systematic R&D documentation is important not only for the R&D Tax Credit but also for other funding programs, tax audits, and internal management of your innovation activities.
