Retroactive Application – Why Now Is the Best Time

The Research Allowance Act (FZulG) has been in effect since January 1, 2020. All fiscal years since 2020 are generally eligible – provided the tax assessment for the respective year is still open or amendable. This means: if you conducted research and development in recent years but never applied for the R&D Tax Credit, you are potentially entitled to a substantial funding amount.

The reality is: a large proportion of eligible companies in Germany have not yet taken advantage of the R&D Tax Credit. The reasons are varied – lack of awareness of the FZulG, uncertainty about own eligibility, insufficient capacity for the application, or the false assumption that R&D activities do not meet the funding criteria. The result: billions in funding remain unused.

Why is right now the best time for retroactive application? Three factors speak for it:

  • Statute of limitations risk for 2020: The assessment period for fiscal year 2020 is about to expire in many cases. Those who do not act now will permanently lose their claim for this year.
  • Cumulative effect: Applying for multiple years at once can yield a six-figure or even seven-figure funding amount in one go – a unique opportunity that will not repeat.
  • SME bonus from 2024: The increased funding rate of 35% for SMEs applies from fiscal year 2024 through the Growth Opportunities Act. You can directly benefit from this with your retroactive application.
Urgent action required: The longer you wait, the more past fiscal years risk expiring. The retroactive application is time-critical – start assessing your funding potential now. NOVARIS offers a free initial analysis to identify which years are still open for you.

Requirements for the Retroactive Application

For a retroactive R&D Tax Credit to be successfully claimed, several requirements must be met simultaneously. The good news: most requirements can also be fulfilled or documented retroactively. Here are the five key requirements in detail:

1. R&D Projects in the Respective Fiscal Year

Actual research and development activities must have been carried out in the relevant fiscal year. The activities must correspond to one of the three categories of the Frascati Manual: basic research, applied research, or experimental development. It is crucial that a technical uncertainty had to be overcome – pure routine activities, even if technically demanding, are not sufficient.

2. Tax Assessment Still Open or Amendable

The tax assessment for the relevant year must still be open – either because it is subject to review, because an objection has been filed, or because the assessment period has not yet expired. In practice, most tax assessments from recent years are still subject to review, so the R&D Tax Credit can be applied for retroactively. Your tax advisor can check the status of individual assessments.

3. Personnel Costs Verifiable

The eligible expenditures – typically gross wages and salaries of R&D employees – must be verifiable for each fiscal year. Relevant documentation includes payroll records, employment contracts, job descriptions, and salary statements. In most companies, these documents are readily available from payroll accounting, even for past years.

4. R&D Character Documentable

The R&D character of the projects must be demonstrated to the Federal Certification Body (BSFZ). This is done through a BSFZ application describing the project, scientific-technical objectives, and degree of novelty. This application can also be submitted retroactively – it is not required that the BSFZ application existed during the research period.

5. Separate BSFZ Application for Each Year

A separate BSFZ application must be filed for each fiscal year. This means: if you want to apply retroactively for 2020 to 2025, up to six separate BSFZ applications are required. A project spanning multiple years can be described as a continuous project across the relevant years.

Summary of requirements:

1. R&D projects carried out in the respective fiscal year
2. Tax assessment still open (subject to review or assessment period)
3. Personnel costs verifiable from payroll accounting
4. R&D character documentable retroactively (BSFZ application)
5. Separate BSFZ applications for each fiscal year

The Biggest Challenge: Documenting R&D Retroactively

By far the biggest challenge with retroactive applications is the retrospective documentation of R&D activities. Many companies have carried out excellent research and development work in recent years but have not maintained separate R&D time tracking or formal project documentation as required by the FZulG.

The good news: A complete reconstruction is possible in the vast majority of cases. R&D activities leave numerous traces within the company that can be compiled retroactively into audit-proof documentation.

Sources for Retrospective Reconstruction

The following documents and data sources are excellent for retroactively documenting R&D activities and reconstructing the time spent by involved employees:

  • Project plans, milestones, and minutes: Internal project management documents such as Gantt charts, sprint plans, milestone reports, and meeting minutes often document the R&D process in detail.
  • Email correspondence and digital communication: Emails, Teams chats, and Slack messages on research topics document the timeline and substantive character of R&D work.
  • Jira, Confluence, Git commits: Software development tools contain detailed records of development work, including timestamps, effort estimates, and content descriptions.
  • Patent applications and publications: Filed patents and scientific publications are strong evidence of the R&D character of work performed.
  • Prototypes and test results: Physical or digital prototypes, test results, laboratory reports, and experimental protocols document the experimental development.
  • Organizational charts and job descriptions: These can be used to determine which employees were assigned to R&D tasks and to what extent.
  • Payroll records and employment contracts: R&D employee personnel costs are fully documented in payroll accounting and form the basis for the assessment basis.

Calculation Example: How Much Funding You Can Claim

To illustrate the potential of retroactive application, consider a realistic example. Take an SME with 8 R&D employees and an average gross salary of EUR 75,000 per year. The company has continuously conducted R&D since 2020 but never applied for the R&D Tax Credit.

Fiscal Year Assessment Basis Funding Rate Research Allowance (Forschungszulage)
2020 600.000 EUR 25% 150.000 EUR
2021 600.000 EUR 25% 150.000 EUR
2022 600.000 EUR 25% 150.000 EUR
2023 600.000 EUR 25% 150.000 EUR
2024 720.000 EUR 35% (KMU) 252.000 EUR
2025 720.000 EUR 35% (KMU) 252.000 EUR
Total 1.104.000 EUR

Explanation of the calculation: The assessment basis for 2020-2023 is 8 employees x EUR 75,000 = EUR 600,000 (pure personnel costs). From 2024, the 20% overhead surcharge is added: EUR 600,000 + EUR 120,000 = EUR 720,000. The funding rate for 2020-2023 is uniformly 25%; from 2024, the SME rate of 35% applies.

Over one million euros in R&D Tax Credit – and many companies do not know they are entitled to this money. The R&D Tax Credit is not a subsidy but a legal entitlement. Every company that conducts R&D and meets the requirements has a right to this funding.

This example is conservatively calculated. In practice, contract research costs (70% of external R&D remuneration) are often added, further increasing the assessment basis. A company additionally paying EUR 200,000 per year to external research partners generates an additional EUR 140,000 in assessment basis per year. Learn more in our guide to contract research.

The owner-operator flat rate for shareholders and sole proprietors (EUR 70/hour, max. 40h/week) can also significantly increase the assessment basis – especially in owner-managed companies where the managing director actively participates in R&D projects.
