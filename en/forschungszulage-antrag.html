Why File a Professional Application?

The R&D tax credit (Forschungszulage / FZulG) is one of the most attractive tax incentives for research-active companies in Germany. However, the application process is two-tiered and complex: First, you must obtain a certificate from the BSFZ (Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage), before you can apply for the R&D tax credit at the tax office.

This dual process involves numerous pitfalls. Typical problems include imprecise project descriptions, incorrect cost calculations, or insufficient distinction between R&D activities and routine work.

Applying for the R&D Tax Credit: The 5 Steps to Success

The application process for the R&D tax credit follows a clearly defined procedure. Below, we guide you through each individual step – from the eligibility assessment to the payout.

1

Eligibility Assessment

The first and most important step: Verify whether your research and development projects meet the criteria under Section 2 FZulG. The law distinguishes three eligible categories of R&D activities:

  • 1. Basic research: Experimental or theoretical work aimed at acquiring new knowledge without direct commercial application. This includes, for example, research into new materials, physical phenomena, or biological processes.
  • 2. Industrial research: Planned research to acquire new knowledge with the goal of developing new products, processes, or services, or substantially improving existing ones.
  • 3. Experimental development: The acquisition, combination, and use of existing knowledge for the planning of new or improved products, processes, or services. This also includes prototyping, testing, and pilot projects.
Common misconception: Many companies believe that only "groundbreaking innovations" are eligible. In reality, it is sufficient if a project involves technical uncertainties and pursues a systematic approach to solving them. Even the further development of existing products can be eligible, provided it goes beyond mere routine modifications.

2

Prepare Project Documentation

The project documentation is the centerpiece of your BSFZ application. A precise, well-structured description significantly increases the probability of success and accelerates processing. The BSFZ requires a detailed presentation for each R&D project, containing the following elements:

  • Project objectives and description: What is to be achieved? What problem is being solved?
  • Technical novelty: What is the state of the art? What constitutes the innovative approach?
  • Technical uncertainties: What challenges exist? Why is the solution non-trivial?
  • Methodology and approach: Systematic approach and planned work steps.
  • Timeline and participating staff: Start date, end date, and personnel resources of the project.
Common documentation mistake: Many companies describe their projects too results-oriented ("We developed product X") instead of highlighting the research process and associated uncertainties. The BSFZ does not evaluate the outcome but whether the path to it constitutes a systematic R&D activity.

Professional documentation takes the BSFZ's internal evaluation criteria into account and ensures that the R&D quality of your projects is presented clearly and convincingly. 3

Apply for the BSFZ Certificate

After completing the project documentation, the application is submitted to the Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage (BSFZ). The BSFZ is an institution of the Federal Ministry of Education and Research and assesses whether the submitted projects meet the R&D criteria.

How the submission works: The application is submitted electronically via the elster.de portal. You need an ELSTER registration for this (most companies already have one for tax filings). In the portal, you fill out the official BSFZ application form and upload the project documentation.

Processing time: The BSFZ typically requires 2 to 4 months for the review. In some cases, processing may take longer, particularly for complex projects or during high application volumes.

BSFZ follow-up questions: It is common for the BSFZ to request additional information or ask follow-up questions about specific projects. These questions must be answered promptly and precisely. A late or inadequate response can lead to rejection of the application. In case of a full rejection, it is possible to file an objection and resubmit the application with improved argumentation.

4

File the R&D Tax Credit with the Tax Office

After receiving the positive BSFZ certificate, you apply for the R&D tax credit at your local tax office. For this purpose, you use the Annex FZ (Forschungszulage), which you submit with your annual tax return or separately in advance.

Required documents for the tax office:

  • The BSFZ certificate for all eligible projects
  • The calculation of eligible expenditures (assessment base)
  • The completed Annex FZ to the tax return

Calculating the assessment base: The eligible expenditures primarily include the personnel costs of employees involved in R&D projects. The R&D proportion of their working time is taken into account. For contract research to external contractors, 60% of the paid fee is recognized as assessment base. From 2026, an additional overhead allowance of 20% can be applied to personnel costs. The maximum assessment base is EUR 12 million per fiscal year.

SME vs. large enterprise: Since the FZulG amendment, small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) receive an increased funding rate of 35% on the assessment base, while large enterprises receive 25%. For SMEs, this means a maximum R&D tax credit of up to EUR 4.2 million per year.
5

Receive the Payout

The R&D tax credit is considered in the tax assessment. This means: The amount is first offset against your tax liability. If the R&D tax credit exceeds your tax liability, the surplus is paid out directly to you – a so-called negative tax. This feature makes the R&D tax credit attractive even for companies that currently generate no or only low profits, such as startups or companies in a growth phase.

Timeline to payout: After submission of the Annex FZ and all documents to the tax office, processing occurs within the regular assessment procedure. Typically, it takes 2 to 6 months after the tax assessment before the R&D tax credit is paid out or offset.

If the tax assessment is incorrect: Should the tax office not correctly account for the R&D tax credit or reduce the assessment base, you can file an objection against the tax assessment within one month. The 7 Most Common Application Mistakes

Avoid these typical pitfalls that repeatedly lead to delays, reductions, or rejections. Each of these mistakes can have significant financial consequences – and can be avoided with professional guidance.

1. R&D project described too vaguely

General formulations like "product improvement" or "optimization" are insufficient. The BSFZ expects specific technical details, measurable objectives, and a comprehensible research approach.

2. No clear distinction from routine work

Not every development activity automatically qualifies as R&D. The documentation must clearly articulate where the technical uncertainty lies and why the solution could not be achieved with existing knowledge. Routine changes, maintenance, and purely administrative tasks are not eligible.

3. Personnel costs calculated incorrectly

Determining eligible personnel costs is complex. Common errors: incorrect R&D time proportion, missing social security contributions in the calculation, or not accounting for the new 20% overhead allowance.

4. Contract research not correctly allocated

Special rules apply when commissioning external research providers: Only 60% of costs are claimable, and the contractor must be based in the European Economic Area. Additionally, it must be clear who is the contracting party and who is the contractor for the R&D.

5. Deadlines missed (retroactive claims)

Retroactive applications are only possible as long as the tax assessment for the respective year is still open. Once the tax assessment becomes legally binding, a subsequent application is excluded. Therefore, act early.

6. BSFZ follow-up questions not answered on time

The BSFZ typically sets a deadline of a few weeks for follow-up questions. If this deadline is not met, the application may be rejected without completing the substantive review. A prompt, technically sound response is crucial.

7. Assessment base not optimized

Many companies do not fully utilize their assessment base. Typically, eligible projects are overlooked, the R&D proportion of individual employees is set too low, or the overhead allowance is not considered. A professional analysis uncovers additional funding potential.

Checklist: Documents You Need

Make sure you have assembled all required documents before beginning the application process. This checklist covers all documents needed for the BSFZ application and the tax office application.

Project Description

Technical details, project objectives, methodology, state of the art, and presentation of technical uncertainties for each R&D project.

R&D Personnel Cost Breakdown

Detailed list of all employees involved in the R&D project with their gross salary costs and respective R&D time proportion.

Time Tracking Records for R&D Staff

Evidence of actual hours worked in the R&D area. Project-based time tracking is recommended but not a mandatory requirement.

Contract Research Agreements

If applicable: Contracts with external research service providers in the EEA, including invoices and payment receipts.

Owner Contribution Documentation

For sole proprietorships: Evidence of the owner's own R&D activity. The flat rate is EUR 70 per hour.

BSFZ Certificate

The certificate issued by the BSFZ confirming that your projects have been recognized as eligible R&D activities.

Annex FZ to the Tax Return

The official form for applying for the R&D tax credit at the tax office, including the calculation of the assessment base.
