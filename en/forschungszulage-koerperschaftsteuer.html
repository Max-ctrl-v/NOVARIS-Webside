R&D Tax Credit (Forschungszulage) & Corporate Tax – How the Offset Works

The R&D Tax Credit is directly offset against your corporate tax – or even refunded beyond it. Here you learn how this works in practice.

Last updated: March 8, 2026 · Reading time: approx. 9 minutes

  • •If it exceeds the CIT liability: direct payout of the difference.
  • •No offset against trade tax or solidarity surcharge.
  • •Especially attractive for companies with low tax liability or losses.

R&D Tax Credit in the Tax System: Where It Applies

The R&D Tax Credit under the FZulG is not a traditional subsidy or grant, but a genuine tax credit. Under Section 4 FZulG, it is assessed within the corporate tax or income tax assessment and directly offset against the assessed tax.

Specifically, this means: The tax office first assesses the corporate tax (for GmbH, AG, UG) or income tax (for sole proprietorships and partnerships) as usual. The R&D Tax Credit is then assessed as a separate notice and deducted from the tax liability.

The decisive advantages over other funding programs:

  • Offset against the tax liability: The R&D Tax Credit directly reduces the corporate tax payable.
  • Refund of excess: If the R&D Tax Credit exceeds the tax liability, the difference is directly refunded – a payout to the company account.
  • Not a grant, but a legal entitlement: Unlike funding programs, there is a statutory entitlement. There is no discretion and no budget that can be exhausted.
  • Independent of the profit situation: Even companies in loss years receive the full R&D Tax Credit as a refund.
Important: The R&D Tax Credit itself is tax-free. It is neither considered when determining taxable income nor does it increase the company's profit. This means: The paid-out R&D Tax Credit arrives 1:1 at the company – without further tax deductions.

Anlage FZ: Application at the Tax Office

The R&D Tax Credit is applied for at the responsible tax office via Anlage FZ. Submission is electronic via the ELSTER portal – just like the regular tax return.

Content of Anlage FZ

Anlage FZ contains all essential information for claiming the R&D Tax Credit:

  • BSFZ certificate: The certificate number from the positive assessment by the Certification Body for R&D Tax Credit (BSFZ). Without a BSFZ certificate, no R&D Tax Credit can be assessed.
  • Eligible expenditures: Detailed breakdown of personnel costs (gross wages and salaries), own contributions from sole proprietors, and contract research costs (60% of remuneration).
  • Assessment basis: The sum of all eligible expenditures, capped at the maximum assessment basis (EUR 4 million until 2023, EUR 12 million from 2024).
  • Requested R&D Tax Credit: The calculated funding amount (25% or 35% for SMEs from 2024).

Submission Timing

Anlage FZ can be submitted together with the tax return, but does not have to be. It can also be submitted separately and independently of the tax return. The only prerequisite is that the BSFZ certificate is already available.

Practical recommendation: Submit Anlage FZ promptly after receiving the BSFZ certificate – even if your tax return is not yet ready. This significantly accelerates the assessment and payout process. Processing time at the tax office is typically 2-4 months.

Offset Against Corporate Tax (Körperschaftsteuer)

The offset of the R&D Tax Credit against corporate tax is straightforward and follows a simple principle: The assessed R&D Tax Credit is deducted from the assessed corporate tax. Depending on the ratio, two scenarios arise:

Scenario 1: Tax Liability Higher than R&D Tax Credit

If the corporate tax liability is higher than the R&D Tax Credit, the allowance reduces the tax payable. The company only pays the difference.

Scenario 2: R&D Tax Credit Higher than Tax Liability

If the R&D Tax Credit exceeds the tax liability, the excess amount is directly refunded to the company. This is particularly relevant for high-growth companies with significant R&D expenditures and comparatively low tax liability.

Scenario CT Liability R&D Tax Credit Result
A: Partial Offset EUR 200,000 EUR 150,000 EUR 50,000 remaining liability
B: Full Offset + Refund EUR 80,000 EUR 150,000 EUR 70,000 refund
C: No Tax Liability EUR 0 EUR 150,000 EUR 150,000 refund
D: Exact Offset EUR 150,000 EUR 150,000 EUR 0 – fully offset

Detailed example (Scenario B): A GmbH has a corporate tax liability of EUR 80,000 in fiscal year 2025. At the same time, a R&D Tax Credit of EUR 150,000 is assessed (based on EUR 600,000 personnel costs x 25%). The R&D Tax Credit exceeds the tax liability by EUR 70,000. This amount is directly refunded to the company account.

Liquidity advantage: The R&D Tax Credit directly impacts your company's liquidity. In Scenario B, not only does EUR 80,000 less flow to the tax office – the company also receives EUR 70,000 as a refund. The effective liquidity advantage is thus EUR 150,000.

Trade Tax (Gewerbesteuer): No Addition

A frequently asked question concerns the trade tax treatment of the R&D Tax Credit. The answer is clear and positive: The R&D Tax Credit is fully exempt from trade tax.

This follows directly from Section 4(3) FZulG, which expressly provides that the R&D Tax Credit is not taken into account when determining trade income. Specifically, this means:

  • No addition: The R&D Tax Credit is not treated as income and is not added to trade income.
  • No impact on the trade tax assessment amount: The trade tax assessment amount remains unaffected by the R&D Tax Credit.
  • No impact on the multiplier: The trade tax burden of the company does not change due to the R&D Tax Credit.
  • No trade tax reporting obligation: The R&D Tax Credit does not need to be separately declared in the trade tax return.
Practical significance: The trade tax exemption of the R&D Tax Credit means that the entire funding remains with the company. At a typical trade tax multiplier (Hebesatz) of 400% and an assessment rate of 3.5%, trade tax liability would reduce the effective funding by up to 14%. Thanks to Section 4(3) FZulG, this additional burden is completely eliminated.

Income Tax for Partnerships

The R&D Tax Credit is available not only to corporations (GmbH, AG) but also to partnerships such as GbR, KG, and OHG. Since partnerships are not themselves subject to corporate tax, the offset occurs at the level of the individual partners against their personal income tax.

Distribution Among Partners

The R&D Tax Credit is allocated to the individual partners according to the distribution key of the partnership. Typically, this corresponds to the profit distribution key in the partnership agreement. Each partner receives their share of the R&D Tax Credit as a credit against their personal income tax.

Specifics for Limited Partnerships (KG)

In a KG, the R&D Tax Credit is distributed among general partners (Komplementäre) and limited partners (Kommanditisten). The distribution key follows the partnership agreement. Important: Even limited partners who are not actively involved in management receive their share of the R&D Tax Credit.

Sole Proprietors

For sole proprietorships, the R&D Tax Credit is directly offset against the owner's income tax. Additionally, sole proprietors can claim the own contribution flat rate (EUR 70/hour, max. 40h/week) as an assessment basis – a significant advantage that can substantially increase the funding amount. More details in our Guide: GmbH vs. Sole Proprietorship.

Note for partnerships: The application for the R&D Tax Credit (Anlage FZ) is submitted by the partnership to the tax office – not by the individual partners. The allocation to partners is made by the tax office as part of the separate and uniform determination (gesonderte und einheitliche Feststellung).

Impact on Loss Carryforwards

One of the most attractive features of the R&D Tax Credit is its complete independence from the company's profit situation. This has far-reaching consequences for companies with loss carryforwards:

  • No reduction of the loss carryforward: The R&D Tax Credit does not reduce the existing loss carryforward. The corporate or income tax loss carryforward remains fully intact and can continue to be utilized in future years.
  • Payout despite losses: Even if the company has incurred a loss in the relevant fiscal year and has no tax liability, the R&D Tax Credit is refunded in full.
  • No offset against the loss carryforward: The R&D Tax Credit is not netted against existing loss carryforwards – it is available to the company in addition.
Particularly relevant for startups: Young companies are frequently in a loss phase while simultaneously investing heavily in research and development. The R&D Tax Credit provides these companies with a direct liquidity injection that flows regardless of the profit situation. A startup with EUR 500,000 in R&D personnel costs and zero tax liability still receives EUR 125,000 (or EUR 175,000 as an SME from 2024) as a direct refund – while retaining their full loss carryforward for the future.

This feature fundamentally distinguishes the R&D Tax Credit from tax benefits such as special depreciation allowances or investment deduction amounts, which only take effect when there is taxable profit. In this sense, the R&D Tax Credit is a "true" incentive that delivers its full impact even in economically challenging phases.
