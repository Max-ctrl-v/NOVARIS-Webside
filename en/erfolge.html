Specialty Chemicals: From Initial Doubt to Payout in 14 Weeks

When the managing director of this specialty chemicals manufacturer first contacted us, he was skeptical. His tax advisor had told him the Research Allowance was “only for universities and large corporations.” That was wrong. The company employed 18 scientists and engineers who had been researching catalytic processes for years – exactly the type of experimental development that the FZulG supports.

The biggest challenge was drawing the line: where does routine production end, and where does research begin? We worked through every single development step together with the project managers. For one project developing a new catalyst for producing bio-based surfactants, the case was clear – there was technical uncertainty, systematic experimentation, and genuine novelty. For other projects we had to look more carefully and cleanly separate the R&D components from process optimization.

In the end, we identified six projects and filed retroactive applications covering five fiscal years. The BSFZ certificate arrived after eight weeks, and the payout from the tax office followed six weeks later. Total result: EUR 1.2 million, applied directly against the company’s corporate tax liability.

Mechanical Engineering: 24 Engineers, Zero R&D Documentation – and Still EUR 1.4M

This mechanical engineering company from North Rhine-Westphalia had a typical problem: their development department with 24 engineers had been working on innovative manufacturing systems with integrated sensors and AI-based quality control for years. But nobody had ever documented this work as R&D or claimed it with the tax office. Their existing tax advisor simply had no experience with the FZulG.

We started with a full-day workshop with the development managers. It quickly became clear: four of the ongoing projects met all criteria for experimental development under the Frascati Manual. The sensor fusion for automated defect detection was technically unresolved – the team was systematically testing different approaches. That is exactly what the Research Allowance is designed for.

The particular difficulty was in allocating personnel costs. Many engineers split their time between R&D work and series production. We introduced a system for precise time tracking and reconstructed the costs retroactively. Result: EUR 1.4 million in Research Allowance, including EUR 280,000 additional funding through the 35% SME bonus that has been in effect since 2024.

Engineering Firm: Getting Contract Research Right and Securing the SME Rate

A specialized engineering firm in Bavaria with 65 employees was developing novel testing methods for 3D-printed components. Part of the research was conducted through a cooperation with a technical university – this made the application more complex, but also more rewarding. With contract research, up to 70 percent of external costs can be credited on top of the company’s own personnel costs.

Management had neither the time nor the capacity to handle the application themselves. We took over the entire process: from the initial conversations with the project managers, through drafting the technical descriptions, to all communication with the BSFZ and the tax office. In total, management only needed to invest roughly five hours of their own time.

As an SME with fewer than 250 employees, the firm qualified for the increased 35% funding rate. Combined with correctly credited contract research and three identified in-house projects, the total funding came to EUR 620,000. The entire process – from initial consultation to the official decision – took four months.

PROCESS

Our Process in Practice

From first contact to payout, the typical timeline is three to five months. Here is what each stage looks like.

1
Week 1–2

Initial Consultation & Potential Assessment

Free initial meeting to determine basic eligibility. We review your company structure, industry, and current projects. Within a few days you receive a first estimate of whether funding is realistic and in what range.

2
Week 2–4

Project Identification & Workshop

Together with your development managers, we analyze all current and completed projects. We identify the eligible R&D components and clearly distinguish them from routine work and series production. This step is critical – typically, more projects are found than initially expected.

3
Week 4–6

Application Drafting & BSFZ Submission

We draft the technical project descriptions, calculate the eligible personnel costs, and submit the application to the Bescheinigungsstelle Forschungszulage (BSFZ). The descriptions must clearly demonstrate technical uncertainty, a systematic approach, and novelty – that is our core competence.

4
Week 6–14

BSFZ Review & Certification

The BSFZ reviews the application and issues the certificate upon positive evaluation. If follow-up questions arise, we handle them directly. Current processing time is six to ten weeks. During this phase, you as the client do not need to do anything.

5
Week 14–18

Tax Office Application & Payout

With the BSFZ certificate in hand, we file the application with the responsible tax office. The Research Allowance is then either offset against corporate or income tax or paid out directly. For most clients, the funds arrive within four to six weeks after certification.
