R&D Tax Credit Payout – When and How Does the Money Arrive from the Tax Office?

You have received the BSFZ certificate and filed the application with the tax office. Now the crucial question: When does the money flow?

The path to payout: Step by step

The payout of the R&D tax credit follows a clearly defined process. From the initial application at the BSFZ to the actual credit to your account, you go through four central stages:

1

File BSFZ application (Duration: 4-8 weeks)

The first step is submitting the application to the Certification Body for R&D Tax Credit (BSFZ). This body examines whether your R&D projects meet the substantive requirements of the FZulG. Processing time is typically 4 to 8 weeks. After a positive assessment, you receive a certificate needed for the next step.

2

Submit Annex FZ (Anlage FZ) to the tax office

With the BSFZ certificate, you submit the Annex FZ (R&D tax credit form) to your responsible tax office. This can be done together with the regular tax return or separately. In the Annex FZ, you specify the eligible expenditures and the assessment basis. Tip: You can also submit the Annex FZ independently without a pending tax return.

3

Assessment by the tax office (Duration: 4-12 weeks)

The tax office reviews your application and issues an assessment notice (Festsetzungsbescheid) for the R&D tax credit. Unlike the BSFZ, the tax office does not examine the substantive R&D quality but rather the arithmetic correctness of the eligible expenditures. Processing time varies between 4 and 12 weeks depending on the tax office.

4

Offset or refund

After the assessment, the R&D tax credit is offset against your income or corporate tax (Körperschaftsteuer) liability. If the credit exceeds the tax liability, you receive the difference as a direct refund – typically within a few days after the assessment to your bank account on file with the tax office.

Practical tip: Submit the Annex FZ as early as possible – ideally immediately after receiving the BSFZ certificate. You do not need to wait for your tax return to be filed. This significantly accelerates the payout process.

Offset vs. direct refund

The R&D tax credit is generally offset against the assessed income or corporate tax (Körperschaftsteuer). There are two scenarios particularly relevant for companies:

Core principle: The R&D tax credit is applied against the tax liability. If the credit exceeds the tax, the excess amount is directly refunded. This is the decisive advantage over other tax benefits: The R&D tax credit is a true negative tax.

Scenario 1: Tax liability exists

If your tax liability is e.g. EUR 80,000 and the R&D tax credit is EUR 50,000, you only pay EUR 30,000 in taxes. The R&D tax credit works as a direct tax reduction.

Scenario 2: Tax liability lower than the credit or zero

If your tax liability is EUR 20,000 and the R&D tax credit is EUR 50,000, you receive EUR 30,000 as a direct payout after offset. With a tax liability of EUR 0, you receive the full EUR 50,000 as a refund.

Particularly relevant for startups: Young companies with losses and no tax liability receive the full R&D tax credit as a cash payout. With up to EUR 1 million assessment basis and 35% SME rate (from 2024), this can be up to EUR 350,000 per year – directly to the company account. Total duration first application3-6 monthsFrom BSFZ application to payout/offset

Factors influencing processing time

  • Complexity of R&D projects: Many or technically demanding projects can lead to queries at the BSFZ
  • Tax office queries: Incomplete applications or atypical expenditures trigger follow-up questions
  • Tax office workload: Seasonal peaks (e.g., after filing deadlines) can extend processing time
  • Simultaneous tax return: If the Annex FZ is filed together with the tax return, processing depends on the entire assessment
  • First vs. follow-up application: The first application at the tax office may take longer as the case worker needs to familiarize themselves

What to do if there are delays

In rare cases, there may be delays in processing by the tax office. Here are your options for action:

Phone inquiry

The simplest first step: Call the responsible case worker at your tax office and inquire about the processing status. Often a friendly inquiry is enough to accelerate the process.

Inactivity appeal (Untätigkeitseinspruch) after 6 months

If the tax office has not decided on your application within 6 months without sufficient reason, you are entitled to file an inactivity appeal under Section 347 of the German Tax Code (AO). This is a formal legal remedy that compels the tax office to process your application.

NOVARIS handles the communication: As your advisor, we conduct all correspondence with the tax office. In case of delays, we proactively escalate and file the inactivity appeal if necessary. You don't need to worry about anything.

Further options in case of persistent delays

  • Supervisory complaint (Dienstaufsichtsbeschwerde): In case of repeated inactivity, a complaint can be filed with the office management
  • Tax court (Finanzgericht): In the most extreme case, an inactivity lawsuit before the tax court is possible (Section 46 FGO)
  • Advance payment adjustment: Alternatively, you can request an adjustment of tax advance payments to achieve at least indirect relief

Special features of the payout

The R&D tax credit has some tax-related special features relevant for financial planning:

Not business income for trade tax (Gewerbesteuer)

The R&D tax credit is not business income within the meaning of the Trade Tax Act. This means: The credit neither increases trade income nor trade tax. It flows tax-neutrally into your company.

No VAT

No VAT is charged on the R&D tax credit. The payment does not constitute consideration for a service and is therefore not subject to VAT. More on correct accounting of the R&D tax credit in our bookkeeping guide.

No impact on loss carryforwards (Verlustvorträge)

The assessment of the R&D tax credit has no impact on existing loss carryforwards. Your losses from prior years remain fully intact and can continue to be offset against future profits.

Offset with advance payments

The R&D tax credit can also be offset with ongoing tax advance payments. After the credit is assessed, you can request a reduction of advance payments from the tax office. This improves your liquidity during the year.

Summary: The R&D tax credit is one of the most tax-advantageous incentives in Germany. It reduces the tax burden, is paid out in cash when there is no tax liability, triggers no trade tax (Gewerbesteuer) or VAT, and leaves loss carryforwards (Verlustvorträge) unaffected. A detailed analysis of the interaction with corporate tax can be found in our guide to corporate tax (Körperschaftsteuer).
